Traveling to Merida, Mexico

Merida is a city in Mexico that is located in the southern state of Yucatan. In this beautiful town you can see a great variety of Mayan culture and architecture, beautiful beaches and incredible cenotes (natural wonders with deep blue crystalline water). These are some of the many places you can visit around the city.

  • Celestun
  • Uxmal
  • Paseo de Montejo
  • Cenotes
  • Merida Downtown

Celestun

Celestun is a fishing village with an important biosphere reserve called Ría Celestun. It's a famous wildlife sanctuary with white-tailed deer, armadillos, crocodiles, ocelots, bush turkeys, ducks, and many birds. The main attraction is the sight of large groups of flamingos and the boat ride through the mangrove forests.

Uxmal

Uxmal is the ruins of an ancient Mayan city that is located in the middle of the jungle. It is considered one of the most important Mayan archaeological sites along with Chichen Itza, and it has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in recognition of its significance. You can enjoy a guided walk in the middle of the archaeological sites and learn more about their culture and history.

Paseo de Montejo

Paseo de Montejo is an important avenue in the city where some of the most iconic buildings and monuments are located. It is one of the most famous meeting points because it has a great variety of restaurants, bars, clubs and museums.

One of its most iconic structures is El Monumento a la Patria or "Monument to the Motherland", a stone monument sculpted entirely by hand by the Colombian sculptor Rómulo Rozo.

Cenotes

The cenotes are one of the most beautiful and interesting natural wonders of Mexico. They are water wells with a depth of 32 to 130 ft that were created by the fall of the meteorite that killed the dinosaurs, which impacted the state of Yucatan. The impact created a ring of around 7,000 cenotes that crosses the state.

For the Mayans, cenotes were considered entrances to the underworld and a point of contact with the god of rain, "Chaac", to whom they offered human and animal sacrifices, as well as relics and clothing.

Many cenotes are open to the public and you are allowed to swim and dive in them, as well as take as many photos as you want. It is a unique experience that you do not want to miss.

Merida Downtown

The center is made up of many buildings that were built during the colonial era. Here you can find the main buildings of the city such as churches, museums, markets, theaters and typical food restaurants where you can try the famous Yucatecan dish "Cochinita Pibil".

Throughout the year there are different cultural and musical events, light shows and tours. A popular event is the demonstration of an ancient Mayan sport called The Ball Game or "Pok‑Ta‑Pok".
